Buffalo Bills Stadium Project -- Februrary 2013

Part of my job at Buffalo Computer Graphics involves digitizing various features of aerial photographs. Buffalo, New York's NFL team was interested in purchasing BCG's emergency management software for keeping track of various situations during games. I was tasked with creating vectors of their parking lots, stadium sections, and locations of emergency phones and safety lights. The deadline was short, but I was able to make some useful shapefiles in about ten hours. Click any image to enlarge.


GEO479 Final Project (GIS for Environmental Modeling) -- May 2012

For my final project, I tackled an issue facing Mt. Hope Cemetery in Rochester, New York. Mt. Hope Cemetery is the largest municipal cemetery in the United States, and one of the oldest. The land it resides on has been virtually untouched since the cemetery's inception in 1838. However, the glacial migration that carved out the shape of this land is now one of the leading causes of its problems: many head stones are being displaced or knocked over (and broken) because they were build on steep inclines.

I created a Prezi presentation to discuss this. The presentation is public, and can be seen here.

You can read the final paper here. It helps to explain the use of the images below.
